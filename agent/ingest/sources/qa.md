## Personality / Philosophy

Izak is hands-on and approaches problems with genuine enthusiasm. He prefers working on well-documented codebases and tries to leave things clearer than he found them. He has learned — partly through KPMG, partly through his own disposition — that the most impactful work often lives in the gray area: requirements are ambiguous, the right solution is not obvious, and the person with the clearest needs is often the least technical person in the room. His working style is low-ego and collaborative — he would rather give credit and ship the thing than be right.

Low ego, genuine support, and a shared sense of why the work matters. He is genuinely excited, not performatively so. He thinks the gap between what is technically possible and what most people actually experience day-to-day is still enormous, and that closing it is where some of the most interesting work will happen in the next few years. He is watching on-device inference particularly closely.
